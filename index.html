Interactive Experience Research

Case Study: Luni app(luni app)

First Impressions & Actions

1.What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

Entering this website, the first thing I noticed was the three-dimensional depth of the index section. Unlike many other pages, Luni’s navigation is crafted using 3D modeling, which looks incredibly cool and offers an immersive experience. The center of the screen isn't just a static image; it’s a constantly morphing, luminous fluid-like 3D core. With even the slightest movement of the mouse, the light and shadows of the entire background shift in real-time. This visual effect feels magical—it no longer feels like a traditional, "flat" webpage for reading information, but rather a dynamic digital space that one can truly "enter."

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Land on homepage and move mouse side-to-side for feedback.
  • Scroll down to trigger the transition to the next scene.
  • Pause to observe auto-playing 3D animations.
  • Click the "Studio" button in the navigation bar.
  • Rapidly scroll to test transition fluidity.
  • Click the footer link to search for contact information.

Engagement & Interaction

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time observing the page transitions. Rather than abrupt jumps, every scroll or click feels like a camera lens gliding through a 3D space. Each major heading is paired with a unique 3D model, which intuitively guides even the most novice users. I found myself repeatedly switching between menu items just to experience that seamless, silky-smooth flow. I spent a considerable amount of time trying to wrap my head around the underlying interaction logic because the connectivity was just so captivating.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Throughout these two minutes, my most frequent action was moving the mouse. While scrolling advances the page, I noticed I was subconsciously wiggling the cursor. This is because the visual feedback is incredibly responsive; the cursor acts like a flashlight, subtly altering the light and shadow wherever it points. This instant feedback makes the site feel "alive"—a tangible space capable of immediate interaction.

Primary Goals & Communication

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

In my view, the core objective of this experience is to establish the brand’s high-end technical image and artistic tone. The site isn't strictly functional; it serves as an introduction to Luni and a visual manifesto of their brand identity. Rather than listing product features, it invites visitors to feel Luni’s obsession with detail and their grasp of cutting-edge concepts. The goal is to "showcase prowess" and "convey an atmosphere," ensuring users remember the brand through sensory experience rather than specific utility.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The experience achieves this through "immersive storytelling." By ditching standard sidebars and dense blocks of text, it forces users to "explore" to find information. Every transition and animation sends a clear message: this is a creative studio that prioritizes aesthetics and refuses to follow the crowd.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

Regarding interaction frequency, I see this as a low-frequency engagement driven by curiosity or brand updates. A typical session might last 3 to 5 minutes, much like viewing a piece of digital art. Users don’t need to log in daily for tasks; instead, they return when they need inspiration, want to see the team's latest work, or when the brand drops a major announcement.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The site encourages "long-term engagement" through its sheer richness of detail. While it looks minimalist at first glance, hovering over different elements or entering sub-pages reveals hidden micro-interactions (like stretching text or gradient color shifts). This "detail-oriented" design suggests an evolving space—the experience changes slightly depending on your cursor’s path, keeping the interface feeling fresh and novel.

Media Context & Feeling

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

It draws heavily from the media format of immersive art exhibitions, placing a high premium on immediate user feedback.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These references suggest that I shouldn't rush through the content. Instead, I should observe the environmental shifts and enjoy the interactive journey along the visual path pre-designed by the creators.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

This format encourages a sense of curiosity and the excitement of exploration, much like playing a game. The comfortable texture of the site creates a psychological suggestion: I’m not just looking at a webpage; I’m admiring a piece of digital craftsmanship. This naturally raises my expectations for the company and its output.

User Reflection

12.What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating aspect was the low efficiency of information retrieval. As a student, when I wanted to quickly find contact details or business descriptions, I had to wait for the transitions to finish. Occasionally, if I didn't control the scroll wheel perfectly, I would skip the content I wanted, which can feel a bit exhausting when you're in a hurry.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part was the fluidity and instant feedback. Every mouse movement triggered a smooth reaction from the background, and every click resulted in a seamless camera transition. These elements provided an immense sense of visual pleasure.
